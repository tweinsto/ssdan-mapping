What is the Housing Stress Index?
The Housing Stress Index is a custom metric created to reflect renter vulnerability in each county. It combines:
  • Median gross rent (higher rent = more stress)
  • Percent of renters spending over 30% of income on rent (also known as the rent-burdened rate — a measure of financial strain)
  • Rental vacancy rate (lower vacancy = more stress because renters have fewer housing options and less leverage)
Each of these is standardized (z-scored), and the Housing Stress Index is the average of the three. Higher values indicate more stress on renters.
